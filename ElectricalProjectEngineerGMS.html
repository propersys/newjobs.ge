Electrical Project Engineer: GMS

Job Description

 

We are looking for several motivated project engineers in monitoring system and controls for our Gas Monitoring Systems (GMS) team. As a project engineer, you will work with our engineering and construction team to develop, implement, and maintain GMS safety and integrity for our large-scale construction projects. You will be responsible for analyzing existing gas monitoring and alarm designs, making cost-effective improvements, conducting research, designing testing methods, completing quality assurance and running diagnostics. We are looking for engineers with excellent analytical and problem-solving skills, strong oral and written communication skills, and extensive practical experience.

 

Responsibilities:

  • Design and evaluate innovative practical solutions for projects and assignments with a focus on cost reduction, safety and reliability.
  • Ensuring life-safety and fire code compliance of the design solutions.
  • Apply routine engineering techniques, practices and procedures in support of operational areas.
  • Provide technical direction and/or activity supervision to contractors and subcontractors as required by specific assignments and projects.
  • Provide onsite support for construction and engineering teams to ensure compliance with safety regulations.
  • Troubleshoot problems and design solutions associated with legacy systems and processes.
  • Prepare detailed designs, standards for testing, and prototype fabrications.
  • Perform other duties, assignments, or projects as necessary.

Required Education and Experience:

  • Bachelor's degree or relevant experience in Mechanical/Electrical Engineering or similar background.

Required Skills:

  • Solid knowledge in control systems, sensors and actuators.
  • Can read BIM and/or CAD drawings.
  • Communicates well in a team.
  • Proficiency with Microsoft Office (Word, Excel, and PowerPoint).

Preferred Skills:

  • Knowledge of mechanical, electrical, and life-safety monitoring and control systems typically used in critical environments.
  • Knowledge of PLC systems.
  • PE experience is a plus.

 

Job Type: Full-time

Work Location: Dresden, Germany

Estimate time to start to work: 4th quarter of year 2024
